Hi, I'm Woni.

I'm a PhD candidate in Bioengineering at the University of Pennsylvania, advised by Dr. Casey Halpern. As an undergraduate, I double-majored in Psychology and Physics at Sogang University in South Korea — training that shaped how I pair behavioral and quantitative perspectives on the brain.

My research focuses on identifying intracranial EEG (iEEG) biomarkers associated with neuropsychiatric disorders characterized by compulsive behaviors — including obsessive-compulsive disorder, binge eating disorder, and substance use disorder — and on investigating the impact of deep brain stimulation (DBS) on iEEG signals. My long-term goal is to elucidate the roles of corticostriatal circuits and interoception in psychiatric symptoms related to motivation, including compulsivity and avolition, and to develop novel mechanism-driven interventions.

My core skill set includes clinical trial management, behavioral task design/administration, and signal processing. I have worked with various iEEG systems, including the RNS and g.tec systems.
